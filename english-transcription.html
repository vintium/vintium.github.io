the phonetic symbols i use for my english :3

the traditional system of IPA symbols for transcribing General American English is not very good, mostly because it inherited all of the problems that the system for traditional British Recieved Pronunciation English had. I learned about how bad that situation is from this video, and I decided to apply the same principles laid out there to American English. I also made some other alterations, such allowing for stressed schwa, as my accent does not distinguish [ʌ] from /ə/ phonemically. I also have the Cot, Caught merger, so that is reflected here, as well as the Merry, Mary, Marry merger.

vowels

I divide the vowels into four categories. The first are the two vowels that have no phonotactical restrictions, /ə/ and /ɑ/. The next are the Checked vowels, which must be followed by an obstruent, nasal, or lateral /l/. Then, there are vowels which must be followed by a glide, the Closing Offlgide vowels. Then, all of the vowels that can occur before /ɹ/ are listed, including the syllabic /ɚ/. Also note that [ɤl] is allophonic to /əl/, and that /əl/ is realized as syllabic [l̩].

consonants

My English consonants are in agreement with General American. This is not particularly surprising as consonantal variation between English dialects is usually minimal.
